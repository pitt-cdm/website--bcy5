Hiroshima, the City of History

Hiroshima is an island city of Japan. Hiroshima is most famously known for its Atomic Bomb Dome, which is a historical monument commemorating the site in which the first ever Atomic Bomb was detonated. Near the dome are also the National Peace Memorial Hall, and the Hiroshima Art Museum.

Hiroshima-style Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyakis are one of the many staple foods of Japan. However, the unique part about Okonomiyakis is that different regions in Japan have their own unique twist in making them, and take great pride in their unique twist. Hiroshima style Okonomiyaki in particular is unique that below the typical Okonomiyaki layer composed of flour and egg batter with cabbages topped with sauce, they add Yakisoba(Japanese tyle stir-fried noodles).

Oysters

Because Japan is an island and Hiroshima is a city found on the coast of Japan, it has access to the freshiest seafood. One popular type of seafood that Hiroshima is known for is their oysters. The oysters are freshly caught and they are cooked or served raw to fit the taste of Japanese residents.

Tsukemen

Tsukemen can be thought of as a "deconstructed ramen". Tsukemen is usually served with a dish of noodles (ramen noodles) with a thick, savory sauce with variety of toppings. Tsukemen is eaten by taking the noodle and dipping it in the thick, soupy sauce. Many different types of dipping sauces of many flavors can be found in a Tsukemen restaurant.
