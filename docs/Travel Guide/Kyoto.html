Kyoto, the City of Culture

Kyoto is a city that's rich in Japanese tradition and culture. As the former capital of Japan for over a millenia, Kyoto is the home to many traditional temples and shrines. Kyoto is also known for its refined and delicate traditional Japanese quisine. Vegetable and tofu, in particular, are very popular in Kyoto.

Tofu

Tofu is a common food item that's widely eaten and loved by Americans. However, they are usually found in stir-fried dishes heavily doused in flavorful sauces. Compared to tofu in America, the tofu found in Kyoto are eaten plain with mildly seasoning. Due to the high quality soybeans used, which are made into tofu using traditional methods, the tofu found in Kyoto are very flavorful and rich.

Wagashi & Yatsuhashi

Wagashi and Yatsuhashi are two unique dessert items in Japan, mostly found in traditional restaurants in Kyoto. Wagashi(left) is a term to describe traditionally eaten Japanese sweets that are often eaten with green tea. Yatsuhashi(right) are especially unique to Kyoto and are commonly sold in decorative boxes as souvenirs. They are a type of dessert made with glutinous rice flour and cinnamon.

Shojin Ryori

Shojin Ryori is a term that describes a traditional arrangement of dishes specially prepared for Buddhist monks. Although it is a meal designed to fit the nutritional and dietary needs for monks, Shojin Ryori is served in restaurants to commoners so that they can experience the intricate yet simple flavors that Buddist monks experience in their prepared food.

Kaiseki Ryori

Kaiseki Ryori is a intricate type of cooking that are usually served in Ryokans(Traditional Japanese style inns). Kaiseki Ryori was initially a meal that was eaten as a part of traditional tea ceremonies. Today, Kaiseki Ryori can be enjoyed by anyone looking to stay a night at a local ryokan.
